# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small to medium Swiss businesses (KMUs) who need a custom-built website and are tired of template solutions. They are practical decision-makers — often owners or ops leads — who care about quality, honest communication, and fair pricing more than agency prestige. They're evaluating whether NT Digital can be trusted with their brand.

## Product Purpose

NT Digital is a two-person Swiss web dev agency (two ICT apprentices, year 2). The site exists to demonstrate credibility, communicate their service offering, and convert KMU leads into project conversations. Success looks like a potential client reading the site and thinking: "These two know what they're doing and I can talk to them directly."

## Brand Personality

Bold, confident, ambitious. Two apprentices who punch above their weight — not because they oversell, but because the work stands on its own. The voice is direct, never defensive about being young. Conviction without bravado.

## References

Linear.app, Vercel — sharp, dark, performance-obsessed. Feels like serious technical work, not a design exercise. The craft is in the precision.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: pastel gradients, wave SVGs, hero metric cards, "trusted by X companies"
- Dark-mode dev portfolio clichés: neon terminal text, matrix effects, too-clever hover tricks
- Template-feeling agency sites: purchased-looking grids, stock photos, every section the same width and padding

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell.** Claims about quality are worthless; the site's own craft is the proof. Typographic precision, spacing rhythm, and motion restraint demonstrate the work ethic before any portfolio piece loads.
2. **Conviction over hedging.** Two apprentices is a fact, not a liability. Never frame youth as something to apologize for. Confident copy, confident layout decisions.
3. **Every element earns its place.** Swiss influence: no decoration for decoration's sake. If something is on the page, it has a job. Remove anything that doesn't.
4. **Technical seriousness, human warmth.** The aesthetic should feel like Linear — precise and dark — but the voice should feel like talking directly to the people who built it.
5. **Restraint as a design choice.** The competition overdesigns. NT Digital wins by doing less, but better.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigation, sufficient color contrast (4.5:1 body, 3:1 large), alt text on all images, reduced motion via `prefers-reduced-motion`. German as primary language.
